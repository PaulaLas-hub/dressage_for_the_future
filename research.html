When Technical Faults May Signal Welfare Compromise: Reinterpreting Common Dressage Descriptors Through Equine Welfare Science

Paula Lasersohn

This paper examines whether the vocabulary dressage uses to describe "technical faults" may, in some cases, be masking welfare compromise. Terms such as "tense through the back," "behind the leg," "behind the vertical," and "lacks harmony" or "submission" are typically interpreted as training deficits to be corrected through more schooling. Emerging welfare science suggests that many of the same presentations may instead reflect pain, discomfort, stress, fear, or maladaptive coping states. The paper synthesises evidence from peer-reviewed literature on the Ridden Horse Pain Ethogram, conflict behaviours, rein tension, oral lesions, restrictive head-neck postures, stress physiology, and dressage competition studies.
